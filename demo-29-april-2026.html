💰 Payment Overview

SCN uses a commitment-based payment system. When a company hires crew, funds are charged to the company's bank account (ACH) or card and held in a holding account until the job is complete. Crew is paid out 7 days after the event ends.

How Batches Work

Batching triggers (in priority order):
  • T-7 Final: Event is within 7 days — force-batch all unbatched hires
  • Threshold: Unbatched hires ≥ 25% of crew needed since last batch
  • Weekly: No batch in last 7 days + unbatched hires exist (anchored at T-14, T-21, T-28)

Retry Logic (NACHA Compliant):
  • Max 2 ACH retries per NACHA rules for transient codes (R01 insufficient funds, R09 uncollected)
  • After failure → grace window opens until the next batch is processed
  • During grace: company can choose to pay via card manually (card button disappears 1h before next batch to prevent race conditions)
  • Next batch trigger picks up the failed commitments with retryCount + 1
  • If still > 7 days to event → ACH retried (cheaper). If < 7 days → switches to card
  • If no future batch window found (already inside T-7) → fallback to fixed 24h grace
  • Idempotency key: retry-{commitmentId}-{attempt} prevents double-charges

Why Card Inside 7 Days?
  • ACH takes 3–5 business days to settle via NACHA / Stripe
  • With <7 days to event, there's risk funds won't arrive before the crew works
  • Card charges confirm instantly — crew hire is secured immediately
  • If card is declined: hire is rolled back, crew is released
  • Card fee is higher: 2.9% + $0.30 (vs ACH 0.8% capped at $5)
  • Company's saved card (backup payment method) is used off-session

Escalation Decision Tree:
  • Batch 1 fails (T-12): ACH fails → grace window opens until next batch → commitments reset to committed
  • Time passes: Next batch trigger fires at T-7 (final_t7)
  • Batch 2 (T-5): System detects retryCount > 0 + daysToEvent < 7
  • ACH retried anyway (NACHA allows one more try since retryCount < 2)
  • ACH fails again: System immediately falls back to card (no waiting)
  • Card charged: Uses stripeCardPaymentMethodId off-session
  • If card also fails:
    • If ≥1 day to event → open manual window, company can retry via UI
    • If <1 day to event → rollback hires, cancel job if no other funded crew

🔧 Manual Card Payments

At any point between batches, companies can use their backup card to make one-off payments. This is available during ACH grace windows and for supplemental invoices after time reconciliation.

When Manual Card is Available:
  • During ACH grace window: After an ACH failure, company sees "Pay Now" button until the next batch fires (card button disappears 1h before to prevent race conditions)
  • Supplemental invoices: After time reconciliation, if crew worked more hours than committed
  • Card option expires 1 hour before the next batch window (prevents race conditions with the ACH cron)
  • Card charged at 2.9% + $0.30 (higher than ACH but instant)

📊 Fee Structure

Every payment includes a platform fee plus Stripe processing. Fees are "grossed up" so the crew receives their full rate.

🏦 ACH Payment — $1,000 crew payout

Crew Payout $1,000.00
Platform Fee (5%) $50.00
Stripe ACH (0.8%, max $5) $5.00
Company Charged $1,055.00

💳 Card Payment — $1,000 crew payout

Crew Payout $1,000.00
Platform Fee (5%) $50.00
Stripe Card (2.9% + $0.30) $30.80
Company Charged $1,080.80
Fee Math — Card Gross-Up Formula:
chargeAmount = (crewTotal + platformFee + $0.30) / (1 − 0.029)
This ensures the platform collects exactly 5% regardless of payment method. The Stripe processing fee is absorbed into the total charge.

🛡️ Safety Guardrails

Built-in protections:
  • Idempotency: Every Stripe call uses unique keys — safe to retry, no double-charges
  • Distributed locking: Prevents two batch processes from racing on the same job (5-min TTL)
  • NACHA compliance: Maximum 2 ACH retries for transient failure codes
  • Card validation: System verifies card is actually type card (not accidentally a bank account)
  • Hard deadline (T-1): If all payments fail within 24h of event → hires rolled back, crew released
  • Grace windows: Company always gets a manual recovery option before automatic escalation
  • Batch splitting: Exhausted retries (≥2) get card batch, fresh ones get ACH — optimizes cost

What's new: Crew members who sync their Google Calendar can now have non-blocking events.   • The popover shows "Google Calendar" source + "Won't block availability" tag
  • Crew's availability stays open for booking during these times
